# Ember privacy policy

*Last updated: July 6, 2026*

Ember is a personal relationship app. Everything you put into it stays on your device. That's the whole policy, but here is the detail:

## What Ember stores

- The people you choose to track, the interactions you log, your notes, cadences, milestones, and preferences
- Names, photos, phone numbers, emails, and birthdays read from the contacts you explicitly select, with your permission

All of this lives in a local database on your iPhone (shared with the Ember home screen widget through Apple's app group container). If you enable iCloud sync in a future version, data would move only through your personal iCloud account, encrypted by Apple, and this policy would be updated first.

## What Ember sends anywhere

Nothing. Ember has no servers, no accounts, no analytics, no ads, no tracking SDKs, and makes no network requests. We never see your data, and neither does anyone else.

## Permissions Ember asks for

- **Contacts**: to link the people you pick to their contact cards so names, photos, and birthdays stay current. Ember never modifies or uploads your contacts.
- **Notifications** (future): to send you a daily digest of who you might reach out to. Generated entirely on your device.
- **Face ID** (optional): to lock the app if you turn that on. Authentication is handled by iOS; Ember never sees biometric data.

## Deleting your data

Delete a person in the app to remove them and their history, or delete the app to remove everything. There is no copy anywhere else.
